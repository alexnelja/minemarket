### Commodity Supply Chain Applications

**Minerals traceability:**
- Glencore's **Re|Source** pilot: end-to-end cobalt tracing from DRC mines to EV production (with CMOC, ERG, Umicore)

- DRC blockchain cobalt monitoring (launched 2018) for artisanal mining traceability

**What works in 2026 (post-failure era):**
- Industry shifted to **"network of networks"** architecture
- Electronic Bills of Lading (eBL): 9 major carriers committed to 100% conversion by 2030
- Legal backing via UK Electronic Trade Documents Act 2023, UNCITRAL MLETR (11+ jurisdictions)
- SWIFT/MonetaGo duplicate financing validation
- Narrow scope + existing infrastructure integration = success

## 4. Legality and Regulatory Status

### United States — Landmark 2025-2026

**GENIUS Act** (signed July 18, 2025):
- First comprehensive federal crypto framework in US history
- Regulates $260B stablecoin market
- Mandates 100% reserve backing
- Federal and state licensing pathways

**SEC/CFTC Joint Interpretation** (March 17, 2026):
- 18 major cryptocurrencies classified as digital commodities (not securities)
- SEC regulates digital securities
- CFTC regulates digital commodities
- Commodity-backed tokens generally fall under CFTC jurisdiction

**CFTC Crypto Sprint** (Aug 1, 2025):
- Permits tokenized assets as collateral
- Allows spot crypto contracts on CFTC-regulated exchanges
- Working to allow futures exchanges to list digital asset derivatives

### European Union — MiCA
- Full implementation: **December 30, 2024**
- World's most comprehensive crypto framework
- Commodity tokens must comply with Asset-Referenced Token (ART) or E-Money Token (EMT) classifications
- Stringent requirements causing "significant market disruption"

### UAE
- VARA + CMA shared framework (Aug 2025), mutual license recognition
- VARA Rulebook 2.0 (May 2025)
- Building frameworks for tokenized RWAs: custodianship, ownership, settlement
- Abu Dhabi FSRA regulates Binance TradFi commodity perpetuals

### Singapore
- New regime effective **June 30, 2025**
- All token service providers serving overseas clients need Part 9 licence or cease operations
- No transitional period — immediate enforcement

### South Africa
- Crypto formally recognized as financial products under FAIS
- **300+ CASPs licensed** by FSCA (of 512 applications)
- Travel Rule effective April 30, 2025
- 2026: clarity expected on stablecoins and enhanced CASP supervision
- **No specific commodity-blockchain trading regulations yet** — general crypto framework applies

### Africa (Broader)
- Most jurisdictions lack specific blockchain commodity trading frameworks
- Rwanda positioning as continental blockchain hub (2025 Africa Blockchain Festival, 1,600+ attendees)
- Kenya, Nigeria: general crypto regulation emerging but no commodity-specific blockchain frameworks
- Key barriers: infrastructure gaps, financial inclusion priorities take precedence

### Why Some Jurisdictions Are Resistant

1. **Securities classification uncertainty** — tokens combining commodity exposure with yield/governance blur the line
2. **Custody/settlement finality** — when does on-chain transfer = legal ownership transfer?
3. **KYC/AML challenges** with pseudonymous public chain wallets
4. **Consumer protection** concerns with 24/7 leveraged commodity trading
5. **Existing commodity futures regulations** designed for physical delivery don't map to tokenized assets

### What Needs to Change
- Legal recognition of electronic documents (UNCITRAL MLETR — 11+ jurisdictions adopted)
- Clear commodity vs. security classification for backed tokens
- Harmonized custody standards across jurisdictions
- Regulatory sandboxes for commodity tokenization pilots

- **Best positioned in Africa** for blockchain commodity trading
- Regulatory framework exists — barrier is liquidity, institutional adoption, and infrastructure
- No commodity-specific blockchain regulations yet (general crypto framework applies)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Tokenized gold is the only mature, liquid commodity on-chain** — $7B+ market, $178B annual volume. Everything else is early stage.

2. **The "big platform" model is dead** — TradeLens, we.trade, Marco Polo, Contour all failed. Survivors (VAKT, Komgo) had narrow scope and genuine founding participant buy-in.

3. **Regulation is rapidly catching up** — US GENIUS Act (2025), SEC/CFTC joint interpretation (Mar 2026), EU MiCA (2024), UAE VARA 2.0 (2025). Clearest regulatory picture in blockchain history.

4. **Africa's blockchain commodity story is traceability, not trading** — driven by international ESG requirements and conflict mineral regulations, not local exchange infrastructure.

5. **Canton Network is the institutional dark horse** — $6T in assets, Goldman Sachs/DTCC backing, privacy-preserving. Worth watching for commodity settlement.

6. **South Africa could operate a blockchain commodity trading platform legally today** under existing FSCA licensing. The barrier isn't legality — it's liquidity, adoption, and infrastructure.
